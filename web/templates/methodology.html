Methodology & Transparency

🔍 Overview

PiiTrade uses a fully automated, no-cost machine-learning pipeline to generate real-time buy/sell/hold signals for 51 trading pairs across four asset classes: forex (majors, minors, and exotics), commodities (gold, silver, WTI oil, Brent oil), major cryptocurrencies (BTC, ETH, XRP, SOL, BNB), and top US stocks (AAPL, TSLA, NVDA, AMZN, MSFT, GOOGL, META).

Signals are updated dynamically using live price feeds from institutional-grade data sources. The model pipeline is designed to be transparent, reliable, and entirely free to use.

📡 Data Sources

  • Historical price data: Years of daily OHLCV (Open/High/Low/Close/Volume) candles sourced from live market data providers.
  • News sentiment: Financial headlines are collected and scored using a natural language processing (NLP) sentiment model that measures market mood.
  • Economic indicators: Inflation data, PMI readings, and interest-rate differentials sourced from government statistical agencies and central banks.

🤖 Machine Learning Model

The model is a gradient-boosted tree classifier trained to predict the next-day direction (BUY / SELL / HOLD) for each pair. Features include:

  • Technical indicators: RSI, MACD, EMA, Bollinger Bands, and momentum oscillators
  • Daily return, short-term and medium-term price momentum
  • Aggregated news sentiment score (rolling window)
  • Macroeconomic data: inflation delta, PMI, yield spread (base vs. quote currency)

The model is retrained regularly on fresh data using rigorous time-series cross-validation to prevent data leakage and ensure robust performance.

⚙️ Automation Pipeline

The end-to-end pipeline is fully automated:

  • Data collection: Live price data and economic indicators are fetched automatically from market data providers on a scheduled basis.
  • News scraping: Financial headlines are collected and scored for market sentiment using NLP models.
  • Model inference: The trained model is evaluated against fresh data and predictions are updated in real time.
  • Alert dispatch: Significant signal changes trigger email notifications to subscribers.

⚠️ Limitations & Transparency Notice

The AI model is a probabilistic classifier: it generates a confidence score derived from predicted class probabilities, not a guaranteed certainty. Individual predictions may be incorrect, especially in volatile markets.

Markets are influenced by events that cannot be captured in historical price data or news sentiment alone — geopolitical shocks, central bank surprises, or sudden policy changes can quickly invalidate model assumptions. The model may underperform during high-volatility or news-driven regimes.
