Technical Analysis for Forex: RSI, MACD, EMA and Bollinger Bands Explained

📅 April 21, 2026 ⏱️ ~12 min read ✍️ PiiTrade Team

Technical analysis is the study of historical price action to forecast future price movements. Rather than analysing a company's financials or a country's economic fundamentals, technical analysts look at charts, patterns, and mathematical indicators derived from past price and volume data.

PiiTrade's AI model is built on technical indicators as its primary feature set. Understanding what RSI, MACD, EMA, and Bollinger Bands actually measure will help you interpret signals more intelligently and identify when the model's confidence may be better or less reliable.

1. RSI — Relative Strength Index

Quick Reference: RSI-14
Period used by PiiTrade14 periods (daily candles)
Range0 to 100
Overbought thresholdAbove 70
Oversold thresholdBelow 30
Neutral zone40–60

What RSI Measures

RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate whether an asset is overbought (potentially due for a pullback) or oversold (potentially due for a bounce). It compares the average gain to the average loss over a look-back period (14 periods in PiiTrade's model).

How to Interpret RSI

  • RSI above 70 (Overbought): The pair has rallied strongly and may be due for a correction or consolidation. Not a sell signal by itself — overbought can stay overbought in strong trends — but a warning to tighten stop losses on long positions.
  • RSI below 30 (Oversold): The pair has fallen sharply and may be due for a bounce. Similarly, not a buy signal alone, but suggests selling pressure may be exhausted.
  • RSI crossing back through 50: Considered a momentum confirmation signal — a cross above 50 suggests bullish momentum is building; a cross below 50 signals bearish momentum.

RSI Divergence (Advanced)

One of the most powerful RSI signals is divergence — when price makes a new high but RSI does not (bearish divergence), or price makes a new low but RSI does not (bullish divergence). Divergence often precedes reversals and is one of the features PiiTrade's model implicitly captures through momentum features.

📊 PiiTrade uses RSI-14 as one of several features feeding into the LightGBM model. It is not used as a standalone trading rule — the model combines RSI with MACD, EMA, Bollinger Bands, momentum, and macro features to generate a composite signal.

2. MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence

Quick Reference: MACD (12, 26, 9)
Fast EMA12 periods
Slow EMA26 periods
Signal line9-period EMA of MACD
MACD HistogramMACD line − Signal line
Bullish signalMACD line crosses above signal line
Bearish signalMACD line crosses below signal line

What MACD Measures

MACD tracks the relationship between two exponential moving averages — a fast (12-period) and a slow (26-period). When the fast EMA is above the slow EMA, the MACD line is positive, indicating upward momentum. The signal line is a 9-period EMA of the MACD line, used to generate trading signals.

Key MACD Signals

  • Bullish crossover: MACD line crosses above the signal line — suggests upside momentum is building. This is often the most watched MACD signal for long entries.
  • Bearish crossover: MACD line crosses below the signal line — suggests downside momentum is building. Used as a short entry or exit signal for longs.
  • Zero-line cross: When MACD crosses above/below zero, it means the fast EMA has crossed above/below the slow EMA — a stronger trend confirmation.
  • Histogram expansion: Growing histogram bars (either above or below zero) indicate strengthening momentum in that direction.
💡 Best use: MACD is most reliable as a trend confirmation tool in trending markets. In choppy, sideways markets it generates many false signals. PiiTrade's model accounts for this by using volatility features alongside MACD to weight signal reliability by market regime.

3. EMA — Exponential Moving Average

Quick Reference: EMA-20 and EMA-50
EMA-20 period20 trading days (short-term trend)
EMA-50 period50 trading days (medium-term trend)
Bullish biasPrice above both EMAs; EMA-20 above EMA-50
Bearish biasPrice below both EMAs; EMA-20 below EMA-50

EMA vs. SMA

An Exponential Moving Average gives more weight to recent prices than a Simple Moving Average (SMA), making it more responsive to recent price changes. For fast-moving forex markets, EMAs are generally preferred over SMAs.

How Traders Use EMA

  • Dynamic support/resistance: In an uptrend, the EMA-20 often acts as a support level where price bounces back upward. In a downtrend, it acts as resistance.
  • Trend filter: Trading only in the direction of the EMA slope is a simple but effective trend filter. If EMA-50 is pointing up, only take buy signals.
  • Golden/Death Cross: When EMA-20 crosses above EMA-50 (Golden Cross), it signals a potential bullish trend shift. When EMA-20 crosses below EMA-50 (Death Cross), it signals a bearish shift.
  • Price vs. EMA: PiiTrade's model calculates the distance of the current price from EMA-20 and EMA-50 as features, capturing how extended or compressed price action is relative to the trend.

4. Bollinger Bands

Quick Reference: Bollinger Bands (20, 2σ)
Middle Band20-period SMA
Upper BandMiddle Band + 2 standard deviations
Lower BandMiddle Band − 2 standard deviations
BandwidthMeasures volatility: wide = high vol, narrow = low vol

What Bollinger Bands Measure

Bollinger Bands are a volatility indicator — they expand when volatility is high and contract when it is low. They are based on a standard deviation calculation around a 20-period simple moving average.

Key Bollinger Band Signals

  • Bollinger Band squeeze: When the bands narrow significantly, volatility is low and a breakout is likely approaching. The direction of the breakout is not predicted by the squeeze alone — confirmation from other indicators is needed.
  • Price touching upper band: In a ranging market, this may suggest the pair is overextended and due for a pullback. In a strong uptrend, price can "walk the band" — touching or exceeding the upper band repeatedly.
  • Price touching lower band: In a ranging market, this suggests potential oversold conditions. In a downtrend, price can walk the lower band.
  • Bandwidth feature: PiiTrade's AI uses Bollinger Band width as a feature to capture the current volatility regime, helping the model weight signals differently in high-volatility versus low-volatility environments.

5. How PiiTrade Combines These Indicators

No indicator works reliably in isolation. PiiTrade's LightGBM model treats RSI, MACD, EMA, and Bollinger Bands not as standalone signals, but as features — inputs that the model weighs against each other based on patterns learned from 10+ years of historical data.

For example, a BUY signal with 75% confidence from PiiTrade might reflect:

  • RSI recovering from oversold territory (35 → 48), indicating momentum recovery
  • MACD line just crossing above signal line on the daily chart
  • Price bouncing off the EMA-20 in an established uptrend
  • Bollinger Band squeeze ending with an upward breakout
  • Positive news sentiment for the base currency in the past 3 days

All five factors aligning produces a strong confluence signal. The model has learned, through 5-fold time-series cross-validation, that this combination of features has historically preceded upward moves in similar market conditions.
